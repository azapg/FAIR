Assignments are the specific tasks you give to your students within a course. They allow you to collect work, provide resources, and manage the grading process.

## Creating an Assignment

To create a new assignment, you must first be inside a course:

1. Open the course where you want to add the assignment.
2. Go to the **Assignments** tab.
3. Click the **Create** button.
4. Enter the **Title** (e.g., "Midterm Essay") and a **Description** with instructions for your students.

## Setting a Deadline

You can set an optional **Due Date** for the assignment. This helps students keep track of their schedule. If you leave it blank, there will be no official deadline.

## Grading Options

FAIR allows you to choose how you want to grade each assignment. When creating or editing an assignment, you can select from these types:

- **Points:** Set a maximum score (e.g., 100 points).
- **Percentage:** Grade based on a scale of 0 to 100%.
- **Letter Grade:** Use traditional marks like A, B, C, or F.
- **Pass/Fail:** A simple way to mark completion or basic competence.

## Adding Resources

If your assignment requires specific files—like a template, a reading list, or a dataset—you can attach them directly.
- Click the **plus (+) icon** in the "Resources" section.
- Upload any files that students will need to complete the task.
- These files will be visible to all students enrolled in the course.

## Managing Submissions

Once the assignment is live, you can click on its name to see the **Submissions** table.
- **View Work:** See a list of all students and the files they have turned in.
- **AI Assistance:** If you have workflows set up, you can use AI to help transcribe, analyze, or draft grades for these submissions.
- **Feedback:** Provide comments and finalize grades for your students to see.

<Warning>Deleting an assignment will also delete all student work and grades associated with it. This action cannot be undone.</Warning>
